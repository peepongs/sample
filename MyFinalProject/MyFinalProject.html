My Learning Journey!

Coding is not my cup of tea..

A little bit about me. I did my undergraduate studies in Electrical & Electronics Engineering, followed by a PhD in Materials Science & Engineering. I currently work full-time—and I’m also a mother to a wonderful 3-year-old daughter. For most of my academic life, I stayed away from coding and computer science—even when it was the most sought-after field. I always believed in hard sciences and mathematics. I told myself, “Coding is not my cup of tea.” I wanted to solve real-world problems by getting my hands dirty, not by sitting behind a screen.In fact, until very recently, I didn’t fully believe in simulations as a precursor to scientific discovery.

What Changed?

One small moment. A colleague of mine used Python to solve a real problem that had been troubling my team for quite some time. Watching that unfold sparked something in me. Around the same time, I came across courses in Data Science and AI—yes, the buzzwords. Curious, I enrolled in a course taught by Prof. Sudarshan Iyengar and learned the fundamentals of Python. That small spark turned into a fire. Even after that, I kept coding at a safe distance. Python just became a tool—something I used to automate parts of my work. But I wasn’t ready to fully step into the world of software development. Then one day, I received an email. A 2-month workshop promising to teach full-stack development using the MERN stack. My first reaction? “That’s impossible.” But then I thought—why not try? So, I applied. And I got in.

Six Weeks Later… A Different Story

I’m now six weeks into the program. Do I claim to know MERN well? Not at all. But the way we learn here is something else—it’s addictive and exhilarating. Within the first two weeks, I completed all the video lectures. I picked up some TypeScript, and I jokingly say I know a bit of React (though I’m still not entirely convinced 😄). But what truly impressed me were MongoDB and Express. There’s something incredibly satisfying about building systems end-to-end. Did I stop there?Not even close. I teamed up with three incredibly driven individuals, and we signed up for an internal hackathon. From someone who once avoided coding… to someone voluntarily joining a hackathon?! That’s a transformation I never imagined for myself. My days are full—with work, responsibilities, and motherhood. But every night, after my little angel falls asleep, I spend two hours learning. Working on milestones. Solving case studies. Building projects. Those quiet late-night hours have become my most meaningful investment in myself. From a student who avoided coding… to someone who now looks forward to building things—This has been a transformation I never saw coming.

And I’m just getting started...
